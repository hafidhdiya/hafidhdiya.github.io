Learning Normality Testing for a Distribution

The article discusses the importance of normality testing in statistical analysis and selecting appropriate methods based on the data distribution. It outlines two approaches: visual (histogram, boxplot, QQ-plot) and numerical (Shapiro-Wilk test, Kolmogorov-Smirnov test, D’Agostino test, Lilliefors test). The choice of method depends on the analysis context. The Shapiro-Wilk test is often preferred for its sensitivity to normal distribution. Visual methods provide graphical insights into data distribution, while numerical methods offer p-values for drawing conclusions regarding data normality.
